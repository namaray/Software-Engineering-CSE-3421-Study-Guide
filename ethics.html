Software Engineering Ethics

Course: CSE 3421 | Framework: ACM/IEEE Code of Ethics

Why This Lesson?

To make software engineering a beneficial & respected profession and ensure efforts are used for good.

The Power of the Engineer: Because we develop systems, we have significant chances to:

  • Do good or cause harm.
  • Enable others to do good or cause harm.
  • Influence others to do good or cause harm.

The 8 Key Principles

Software engineers shall adhere to these eight principles jointly approved by the ACM and IEEE-CS.

1 Public

Act consistently with the public interest.

  • Always put public interest first.
  • Disclose any software-related dangers.
  • Approve only safe and well-tested software.
  • Respect diversity.

2 Client & Employer

Act in best interests of client/employer (consistent with public interest).

  • Honor confidentiality.
  • Only provide services where competent.
  • Accept no outside work detrimental to the primary employer.
  • Use property only as authorized.

3 Product

Ensure products meet the highest professional standards.

  • Ensure goals are achievable and estimates realistic.
  • Strive for high quality, acceptable cost, and reasonable schedule.
  • Ensure adequate documentation, testing, and debugging.
  • Use data legitimately.

4 Judgment

Maintain integrity and independence in professional judgment.

  • Do not accept secret payments.
  • Avoid deceptive financial practices.
  • Disclose unavoidable conflicts of interest.
  • Maintain objectivity.

5 Management

Managers/Leaders must promote an ethical approach.

  • Assign work according to competence.
  • Offer fair and just remuneration (pay).
  • Do not prevent a subordinate’s promotion.
  • Do not ask anyone to breach this code.

6 Profession

Advance the integrity and reputation of the profession.

  • Place professional interest before personal interest.
  • Promote an ethical environment.
  • Associate with reputable people.
  • Share knowledge with other professionals.

7 Colleagues

Be fair to and supportive of colleagues.

  • Assist in professional development.
  • Give full credit to others' work.
  • Do not hinder a colleague’s career.
  • Review others' work only with consent.

8 Self

Participate in lifelong learning.

  • Improve professional skills and knowledge.
  • Improve ability to produce quality work.
  • Understand policies and legislation.
  • Do not violate this code.
